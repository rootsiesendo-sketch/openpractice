The reality without OpenPractice

A process designed
to confuse you

Interstate dental licensing involves up to 10 separate organisations, each with their own portal, timeline, and requirements. Most dentists piece it together from PDFs and forum posts.

01
No single source of truth
Requirements are scattered across state board websites, NERB, IdentoGo, NPDB, and DEA. Each has different instructions and timelines.
02
Direct-send credentials get lost
Your transcript, NERB scores, and license verification must be sent directly from institution to board. Dentists routinely miss this and delay their application by months.
03
The fingerprinting trap
Background check results take 3–4 weeks to reach the board. Most dentists book this too late and push their license date back by a full month.
04
Every state is different
California requires WREB scores. Texas requires a jurisprudence exam. Colorado is one of the fastest; New York is one of the slowest. There's no map.
